01 — What Shram Is
"You had a call yesterday. You promised to send a revised proposal but you forgot. Three days pass. The conversation goes cold. Not because you didn't care — but because no one reminded you."
— Shram.ai homepage

Shram is a Mac desktop AI agent that eliminates the professional cost of forgotten follow-ups. It passively watches your Gmail, WhatsApp, and Google Calendar — no API integrations, no setup — and surfaces actionable, context-aware follow-up drafts before a relationship goes cold.

The core insight: follow-up failure isn't a motivation problem. It's a cognitive load + recollection problem. Shram removes both.

01
Finds

Watches your screen activity across Gmail, WhatsApp, Calendar. Detects missed responses, cold threads, unscheduled meetings.

→
02
Drafts

Uses an on-device memory layer — context from all past activity — to write a personalized, non-generic follow-up reply.

02 — Product Anatomy

Four core systems, one loop

👁
Layer 1

Screen Observer

Shram reads your screen natively on macOS — no OAuth, no API keys. This is both its moat and its friction point. Works OOB from day one.

🧠
Layer 2

On-Device Memory

Context accumulated from all activity over time. Knows that your proposal two months ago was at ₹8L/mo. Remembers tone, history, names. Privacy-preserving by design.

✍️
Layer 3

Draft Engine

Generates personalized follow-up drafts. Not template fills. Mirrors your voice, references actual conversation context, flags correct timing.

⚡
Layer 4

Desktop Execution Agent

Clicks and sends on your behalf via macOS automation. One button from task to done. This is the differentiator vs. pure CRM tools that only remind you to act.

What Shram monitors

  • Gmail (threads where you owe a reply)
  • WhatsApp (business/personal convos on desktop)
  • Google Calendar (meetings with implied next steps)
  • More channels listed as "coming soon"

What Shram intentionally avoids

  • Manual task creation (anti-Todoist thesis)
  • API-first integrations (zero setup philosophy)
  • Team/org features (individual-first)
  • Windows support (Mac-only, for now)
03 — Ideal Customer Profile

Who Shram is for

Shram's own copy says: "If your work runs on relationships that need to keep moving, Shram is for you."

Primary ICP

Founders, sales professionals, consultants, and senior ICs whose daily output = relationship momentum. People who close deals, manage clients, or drive partnerships — where a delayed follow-up = lost revenue.

Behavioral signal

They have 30–80 open conversations across channels at any time. They already use multiple tools (CRM, task manager, email) but still drop balls. They're not disorganized — they're overloaded.

Platform constraint

Mac-only. This is a deliberate early-stage moat: macOS automation APIs are more mature, the ICP (tech-adjacent professionals, founders) skews Mac-heavy, and it filters for high-intent users.

Anti-ICP

Ops/back-office teams, developers (low relationship surface area), Windows-only users, and heavy enterprise orgs with Salesforce CRM mandates.

05 — Honest Gaps & Risks

What needs work

Risk · Privacy perception

Screen reading = trust barrier

"It watches my screen" is a hard sell. Shram needs a clear, visible on-device processing story. Key messaging: nothing leaves your device. Trust page, open security audit, and privacy FAQ are non-negotiables before PMF.

Risk · Platform lock

Mac-only limits TAM

~30% of Indian professionals are on Mac vs. ~70% Windows. The current constraint is intentional but needs a clear Windows roadmap to justify investor narrative.

Gap · Channel coverage

Slack, Teams, LinkedIn missing

Many professionals' highest-value follow-ups live in Slack DMs and LinkedIn messages. The "coming soon" integrations need to ship fast or competitors will close the gap.

Gap · Feedback loop

No explicit learning signal (visible)

The site says "it learns from you" but the feedback mechanism isn't clear. Users should be able to approve/reject/edit drafts and have that improve future drafts visibly.

Gap · Monetization

No pricing visible anywhere

Still in "request access" stage. Without a pricing signal, it's hard to validate willingness to pay. Even a waitlist with pricing teaser would help validate ICP and inform fundraising narrative.

Risk · Frontier labs

OpenAI/Google could ship this

Operator + Gmail context could approximate Shram at scale. The window is 12–18 months. Shram needs proprietary memory quality and distribution before that. Speed is the moat right now.

06 — Growth Levers

How Shram should grow

Acquisition

Founder-to-founder distribution

Target Indian startup founder communities (YC India, Antler, Titan Capital networks, Bangalore founder WhatsApp groups). Founders are the exact ICP — relationship-driven work, Mac-heavy, pain is visceral. Word-of-mouth in this cluster moves fast.

Acquisition

Bottoms-up PLG via "sent by Shram" signature

Every follow-up sent through Shram carries a subtle "powered by Shram" footer (opt-in). Viral loop: the people receiving beautifully-timed follow-ups become prospects. This is the Superhuman/Loom playbook.

Retention

Weekly relationship health digest

Every Monday, Shram sends a "Your relationship health this week" — X follow-ups caught, Y sent, Z conversations that haven't moved in 14 days. Makes the value visible and creates a habit anchor.

Retention

Memory quality as retention

The longer users stay, the richer the memory layer, the better the drafts. This is a compounding switching cost. Shram should make this explicit: "Shram has 4 months of your relationship context. Here's what it remembers about [person]."

Revenue

Freemium → Pro model

Free tier: 10 follow-up drafts/month, 30-day memory. Pro ($15–20/mo): unlimited drafts, unlimited memory, WhatsApp execution, and Slack channel (when available). Annual plan at 2-month discount.

Revenue

Team tier for small agencies/sales orgs

At 5–15 person teams, managers want to see follow-up metrics across the team. Shared contact context + team view = $30–50/seat/mo. This is the PLG → enterprise motion: individual adoption drives team buy.

Moat-building

Memory graph = relationship OS

Over time, Shram has a unique relationship graph per user: who matters to them, conversation history, deal stages, communication cadence. This data layer is defensible and enables a future "relationship intelligence" surface — far beyond follow-ups.

Moat-building

Content-led SEO around follow-up culture

"How to follow up without being annoying", "the cost of a missed follow-up", "best follow-up templates for B2B" — Shram Daily (their news page) is underutilized. This should be a content moat targeting exactly their ICP's search intent.

07 — Future Scope

What Shram can become

Shram's core loop is solid. The opportunity now is to deepen the existing experience and expand the surface area — new channels, smarter memory, and entirely new feature categories that grow naturally from the relationship data Shram is already accumulating.

Improving what already exists
🧠
Memory Layer

Visible, editable memory

Right now, Shram's memory is a black box. Users should be able to see a "memory card" per contact — what Shram knows about them, key promises made, last interaction date. Let users correct or add context manually to improve draft quality.

✍️
Draft Engine

Multi-tone drafts + user voice learning

Generate 2–3 draft variants per follow-up (direct, warm, brief). Track which variant users pick. Over time, Shram learns your exact tone per context — investor emails vs. client check-ins vs. casual intros — and stops offering alternatives.

⚡
Execution Agent

Smarter action types beyond "send"

Currently Shram sends emails/messages. Expand to: schedule a Google Meet, create a calendar block, add a contact to CRM, share a document link — all from the same one-click task interface. Follow-up is rarely just a message.

👁
Screen Observer

Priority scoring on surfaced follow-ups

Not all missed follow-ups are equal. Add a priority signal — how warm is this lead, how recent was the conversation, how important is this person to your work — and surface the top 3 daily, not 20 at once. Reduce cognitive load, increase action rate.

New features to build
📊
New Feature

Relationship Health Dashboard

A visual map of your top contacts — color-coded by last interaction recency, relationship warmth, and pending follow-ups. Gives users a real-time "CRM without the CRM" view. Weekly report: who went cold this week, who's responsive, who needs a reactivation.

💬
New Feature

Slack + LinkedIn + Teams monitoring

The most high-value professional follow-ups often live in Slack DMs and LinkedIn messages — neither of which Shram currently covers. Desktop screen-reading can extend to these surfaces without API integrations, keeping the zero-setup philosophy intact.

🗣️
New Feature

Meeting debrief → follow-up auto-generation

After a Google Meet or Zoom ends, Shram reads the calendar event + any transcript/notes available and auto-generates a post-meeting follow-up: summary, action items, next step — ready to send in one click. No manual note-taking required.

🔔
New Feature

Relationship re-activation nudges

Shram detects contacts you haven't spoken to in 30/60/90 days and surfaces a gentle "reconnect" prompt — with context on what you last discussed and a drafted warm-up message. Turns cold contacts into active ones before they're lost entirely.

📎
New Feature

Smart attachment suggestions

When Shram drafts a follow-up referencing "the proposal" or "the deck", it should surface the relevant file from Google Drive / local disk automatically and attach it to the draft. Closes the gap between "I'll send it" and it actually being sent.

🌐
New Feature

Windows support

Mac-only is a smart early constraint, but it limits TAM to ~30% of Indian professionals. A Windows desktop agent — even in beta — opens up the remaining 70%, especially in enterprise and mid-market where Windows dominates. This is the single biggest TAM unlock available.

🤝
New Feature

Shared context for small teams

For 2–5 person founding teams or sales pods: shared relationship memory. If your co-founder spoke to an investor last week, you shouldn't be reaching out without context. Shram becomes the team's shared relationship OS, not just a personal tool.

🔗
New Feature

CRM sync (HubSpot / Pipedrive)

Push completed follow-ups and contact interaction logs into HubSpot or Pipedrive automatically. This makes Shram a CRM enrichment layer — not a replacement — which enterprise sales teams will pay a premium for. Also enables a logged audit trail per contact.

Long-term vision
"Shram starts as a follow-up tool. It ends as the professional relationship graph for every knowledge worker — the only system that knows what actually happened between people, not what got manually logged."

Relationship OS

  • Know which relationships are growing vs. cooling across your entire network
  • Surface "hidden connectors" — contacts who can intro you to others you're trying to reach
  • Pre-meeting briefs: "You're meeting Rahul tomorrow. Last discussed: Series A deck. He owes you a term sheet update."
  • Life-of-relationship analytics — how has your relationship with a key investor evolved over 2 years?

Proactive intelligence layer

  • Shram detects when a contact gets promoted, raises funding, or posts a job — and prompts a congratulatory reconnect
  • Cross-referencing public signals (LinkedIn activity, news) with private context (your conversation history)
  • Suggests the best time to reach out based on your recipient's historical reply patterns
  • Eventually: voice interface — "Shram, what do I owe to people I met at the summit last month?"
08 — The One Big Bet

What Shram should double down on

"Memory quality is the product. Everything else is the interface."

The follow-up agent is the wedge. The relationship memory layer is the business. Every tool in the market — CRM, task manager, email client — knows what you tell it. Shram knows what actually happened. That's the structural advantage no incumbent can easily replicate.

The risk is shipping too many features before the core memory is magical. A user who experiences "Shram remembered that I promised to send the Annexure 3 from our call 6 weeks ago" will never churn. That's the bar. Build to that, not to feature parity.

🎯

North Star Metric

Follow-ups executed per active user per week. If this compounds over 90 days, Shram is working. Target: 5+ per user/week by month 3.

🏆

The winning thesis

Shram becomes the first tool professionals open in the morning. Not because it's a task manager — but because it tells them what their relationships need today. That's a new category: relationship operating system.

⚠️

The failure mode to avoid

Becoming another reminder tool that users disable after a week because drafts aren't good enough. Memory quality must hit a high bar in beta before any growth push. Bad drafts = trust death.
